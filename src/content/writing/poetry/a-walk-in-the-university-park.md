<!--I decided to go to the university park with my avacado coconut smoothie. To listen to the students—who are really guitarists or harmonicists or singers or musicians. They do go university, but their songs and practice sessions do not give away their youth.

I wish I could witness their playing in class, to see them anxious. Or to witness their playing at the recital, to see them proud. Or maybe I mixed those. The entire hall would watch, surrounded by friends and family, but most importantly the strangers who are listening for the very first time. Who've spent money. Begging to witness something revelatory or to be condemned or exalted by the music, or whatever feeling someone has when they witness something that transforms them.

The idea is that they'd remain silent for the rest of the night. Or at least certainly on the walk home while they attempt to resolve some troubling detail that's been chewing at them. Think of a pearl that is born from the music and grows and matures alongside the progression of the performance. This glowing thing would float above the audience. Then, as only to be matched by the climax of the performance, would break apart into many little pieces, each of which seeking out a place in an observer. This would occur again the following night, and the following.

Some people wait outside the venue for a chance to see the performer and to get a signing, or to observe them a bit more. Doing so is very dangerous and not advised.

The condensation from my avocado coconut smoothie filled up my hand as I walked. It reminded me of the times I have performed: clammy hands.

-- Ethan Perry-->
